# Business-Analytics-Project-Sales-
Sales Performance Analysis Project 

Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing retail sales performance across regions, product categories, customer segments, and time periods to transform raw sales data into actionable business insights. Using structured sales data from a Kaggle dataset and IBM Cognos Analytics, the objective was to identify performance trends, uncover market gaps, and support strategic business decision-making through interactive dashboards and reports. Based on the uploaded presentation, the analysis covered over 10,000 sales transactions across multiple variables including sales, profit, quantity, region, category, and order date.


Business analytics

Business Problem

Retail management lacked clear visibility into:
High-performing regions
Product category contribution
Sales trends over time
Profitability and customer behavior
The project solved this by converting unstructured transactional data into a meaningful dashboard for performance evaluation and strategy development.

Data & Tools Used
Dataset Source: Public Kaggle retail sales dataset
Data Size: 10,195 rows × 15 columns
Tools: IBM Cognos Analytics, Excel
Processes: Data cleaning, formatting, validation, dashboard development

Key Analytical Insights

1. Category Performance
Technology emerged as the strongest category with the highest sales and revenue contribution.
Furniture showed moderate performance.
Office Supplies had high quantity movement but relatively lower revenue, indicating lower margins.

3. Regional/Country Performance
The United States dominated sales performance with overwhelming contribution.
Canada contributed very little, revealing heavy market dependency on one geography.
This concentration creates business risk due to lack of diversification.

5. Time-Based Sales Trends
Sales peaked in 2024 with the highest overall performance.
Monthly sales showed strong spikes but lacked consistency.
Revenue volatility suggests seasonal or campaign-driven dependency.

Dashboard Highlights

Sales, quantity, and profit all exceeded targets.
Profit outperformed most significantly with 46% growth above target.
Consumers represented 50% of total sales, making them the largest customer segment.
Profitability improved faster than revenue growth, indicating stronger operational efficiency.

Strategic Risks Identified

Overdependence on the US market
Weak international market penetration
Customer mix imbalance
Thin margins in certain product categories
Unstable monthly sales cycles

Business Recommendations

Expand into Canada and other markets through localized strategies
Strengthen B2B/corporate sales channels
Improve Office Supplies pricing strategy
Launch customer retention and loyalty programs
Introduce premium technology products for higher profitability
Build omnichannel sales infrastructure for global scalability

Core Business Impact

This project demonstrates how business analytics can move beyond reporting to strategic planning by identifying revenue drivers, operational inefficiencies, and future growth opportunities. It highlights practical skills in:

Data cleaning

Final Insight

The analysis revealed that while the business is profitable and exceeding targets, long-term sustainability depends on geographic diversification, stronger margin optimization, and more stable recurring sales models. This project showcases the power of analytics in turning data into strategic competitive advantage.
